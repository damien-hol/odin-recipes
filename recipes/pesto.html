Pesto recipe

Basil Pesto

To me, pesto is one of those food items that everyone loves but don't know how simple it is to make.

Forget about the official recipe that require pine nuts and peccorino or parmesan cheese that you would need to buy in order to make it!

To me, pesto can be made with pretty much any hard cheese and as for the nuts?

You don't even need them!!

I mean sure, I love the texture and the flavor of almonds, walnuts or even hazelnuts in my pesto.

But if you don't have any of those in your kitchen or someone around you is alergic, you can very easely ommit them and your pesto could still be the best you've ever made so far.

For an amazing pesto

You would need:

  • Fresh basil
  • Fresh garlic
  • Lemons
  • Olive oil
  • A handfull of nuts (optionnal)
  • A fair amount of your prefferd hard cheese

    my favorites are

    • Mozzarela mixed with chedar for a pizza or pasta
    • Swiss cheese to go with a salad
    • Parmesian and peccorino if i feel like being authentic
  • Salt and pepper

As for tools

  • A blender (or mixer)
  • A lemon squeezer or a fork to get the juice out (please try to avoid the seeds in the blender...)
  • A spatulla and a container

Now that we have everything ready,

Lets start

You will notice that i didn't give you any proportions as pesto should be made with love, not with mesurement units

That said, free yourself from the control freakyness comming from all those cooking books and find your inner chef who works with guts rather then a mesuring cup

From a big pack of basil, I would use:

  • One lemon
  • Two garlic cloves
  • A small handfull of nuts
  • And big handfull of cheese
  • A pinch of salt and pepper (make sure to taste the cheese before adding salt, i usualy don't add any salt at all)
  • And two or three onces of olive oil

This list is really approximate as I personaly don't care for lemon and I love cheese, it really should be different between one person to another

How to proceed:

  1. After washing the basil, throw it in the blender
  2. Add in the lemon juice, the olive oil, the garlic, the nuts, cheese and salt and pepper
  3. Close the lid of the blender and push the button.
  4. After everything is mixed together, make sure to tate the pesto
  5. Adjust it adding salt or lemon or oil if needed
  6. Pour it all in the container
  7. Et Voila!!

for better conservation, I recommand a layer of olive oil on the pesto when in the fridge, it protects it from oxidation.
