How to make Crepes

Comming from Britany, France, they are an important part of my life, everywhere I go in the world, I get to make and share with friends a plate of crepes.

I always get surprised to see how many people are unaware of their origin.

I also get surprised of the reaction when trying them.

As it seems, a good crepe is apparentely hard to find outside of a proper "creperie Bretonne" and most of the world have not experienced the simple yet amazing flavors of a tipical crepe Bretonne

Here is how I make it

As a disclamer, even though I grew up in Britany, my parents did not, my recipe is based on friend's familly traditionnal recipes and techniques

For a decent stack of crepes, you need:

  • 250gr of flour
  • 3 eggs
  • 2/3Tbs of sugar
  • About 50gr of salted butter the quality of the end product is highly affected by the quality of the butter you use.
  • about 1/2 liter of milk

how to proceed:

  1. In a pot, melt the butter on low fire
  2. In a big mixing bowl, mix the the flour and sugar
  3. In a small bowl, whisk the eggs
  4. Ones the butter is melted, add a little milk in it so that it doesnt cook the eggs when you mix them together
  5. Ones the butter is melted but not burning hot, mix the butter with the eggs
  6. Do like a little hole in the middle of the flour and sugar mixed together and pour a little bit of the egg an butter mix
  7. Add the rest of butter/egg mix and the milk gradualy to avoid crumbs
  8. Ones everything is mixed well, take a ladle and oxigen the batter
  9. do so by filling the laddle with batter bringing it 20/30 centimiter high and pouring it back in the bowl (without making a mess if possible) repeat the operation for about 2/3 minutes
  10. you are now ready for the cooking process

Don't worry too much if your batter is the right constency, it takes experience to know how it should be before cooking the first crepe, the first crepe bieng the test subject that will allow you to adjust the amount of flour or milk to add.

The cooking process:

As I have had the opportunity to make crepes in friends kitchens while traveling, I've had to addapt and work with the pans that they have.

I am happy to annouce that a "crepe pan" is absolutely not needed and on the contrary... some of those pans are sold in tourists traps and are much more impractical to use then a good old non stick pan that you can pretty much found in any household

Rather then explaining a process to follow, I will present to you my optimum setting knowing that anyone can adapt from that

  • My favorite pan to work with is a lightweith non stick one
  • Cooking on gas to get the temperature very high when needed
  • A little bowl with olive oil and I use a paper towel to spread the oil on the pan
  • A wood or plastic spatula that has a flat head, that is thin and flexible enough to bring it under the crepe without breaking it
  • Two plates:
    • one to place the cooked crepes
    • one to place the crepes that you messed up so that you can eat them right away(some people call it greedy but it's really just to hide the evidence)
